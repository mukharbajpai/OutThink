Decision Logic · PDF Aligned
TAM → SAM → SOM
TAM is the full India ADAS opportunity by 2030 at $456M. SAM narrows this to the most serviceable focus segments at $124.48M. SOM is the realistic early capture at $12.44M.
Segment logic
The rollout prioritizes premium passenger vehicles, commercial fleets, and ride-hailing or taxi fleets because safety ROI, feature willingness, and daily use intensity are stronger here.
Cost logic
The proposal is vision-first and fusion-ready. That lowers BOM versus radar-heavy alternatives and supports a target ASP of $360 while keeping room for localization.
Financial logic
The model assumes phased scale-up, break-even at about 80k units annually with 20% localization, 18% IRR, $35M–$55M NPV, and payback in about 4 years.
Map logic
State colors represent rollout readiness, not just size. The map favors corridor quality, urban density, OEM access, and early commercial fit for L&T deployment partnerships.
